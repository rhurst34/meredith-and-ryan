Travel & Lodging

Travel

Booking Flights

Getting to the Amalfi Coast requires a little planning, but the destination is worth the journey. Here are the best strategies we have found for booking flights.

Direct Route into Naples

The closest airport is Naples (NAP). If you can find a good connection here, it is the easiest option because you are only about an hour's drive or ferry ride from Sorrento.

Route into Rome

We also recommend flying into Rome (FCO) as an alternative. It usually offers more direct flights and better prices. From Rome, you simply hop on the high-speed train (Frecciarossa) to Naples, which takes just over an hour.

Be Creative with Routing

Don't be afraid to break up your trip! Sometimes it is significantly cheaper to book a "positioning flight" to a major hub first.

Via Europe: Look at flying into major cities such as London, Dublin, or Barcelona, spending a night or simply connecting there, and then grabbing a cheap budget flight to Naples.
Via the US: Double check the price of booking a separate domestic flight to JFK (New York) first. Often, the round-trip fares from JFK to Italy are unbeatable.

Passports

A reminder to check that passports are valid for at least 6 months past the travel date.

Italy Tips

Things to Know Before You Go

Download WhatsApp

WhatsApp is the standard way to communicate with everyone in Italy — from your driver to the boat captain. It works over WiFi/Data so you won't get charged for international texts.

Carry Cash

While most restaurants take credit cards, always carry 50–100 Euros in smaller bills. You will need it for tipping, taxis (whose card machines are often "broken"), and spontaneous lemon gelato.

Smart Shoes

The Amalfi Coast is beautiful but steep. Between the cobblestones and the stairs, stilettos are a safety hazard. We highly recommend block heels, wedges, or dressy sandals.

Book Reservations Early

This is a popular region! If you see a restaurant on this list (or Instagram) that you are dying to try, please book it 2–3 months in advance.

Power Adapters

Italy uses Type L (three pins in a row) or Type C (two pins) plugs. Pick one up before your trip!

Our Italy Guide

We have curated a list of our absolute favorite experiences to help you make the most of your trip. Whether you are here for a few days or a full week, we hope you fall in love with this place just as much as we have.

A Private Boat Day Around Capri

If you only do one extra excursion, make it this one. There is nothing quite like seeing the coast from the water. We recommend booking a private boat to drive around the island of Capri, swim in the grottos, and see the Faraglioni rocks up close. It is truly magical.

We booked our experiences through Feeling Italy and highly recommend them! They took amazing care of us.

Beach Club Day at La Conca del Sogno

La Conca del Sogno is a stunning beach club and restaurant tucked away in a secluded cove in Nerano. It feels exclusive, relaxing, and incredibly fun. The food is fresh, the water is clear, and the vibe is unmatched.

Tip: You will need to hire a driver to take you here (it is off the beaten path and tricky to find on your own), but trust us — it is awesome and worth every mile.

An Afternoon in Positano

Positano is the jewel of the Amalfi Coast. We love spending an afternoon wandering the steep, winding streets, shopping for ceramics, and soaking in the views.

The Perfect Nightcap: After exploring, head to Aldo's Cocktail Bar at the iconic Le Sirenuse hotel. The cocktails are incredible (and strong!), but the view of the village houses lit up at night is the real star.

Florence & Rome

A Day Trip to Cinque Terre

There is nothing quite like seeing these five colorful fishing villages stacked against the rugged cliffs. We recommend taking the train from Florence to the first village, Riomaggiore, and then hopping on the local ferry to hop between Manarola and Vernazza. Seeing the coast from the water is truly magical and one of the most special places in Italy!

Steak & Red Wine in Florence

You cannot leave Florence without experiencing a proper Bistecca alla Fiorentina—a massive T-bone steak grilled over wood fire. Here are a few places we recommend: Buca Lapi, Trattoria Sostanza, and Trattoria 13 Gobbi.

Sunset in Rome on Gianicolo Hill

If you want the absolute best view of Rome, head here. Perched just above Trastevere, this spot offers a sweeping panorama of the entire city. We recommend grabbing a bottle of wine and finding a spot on the wall to watch the sunset — you can even spot the Colosseum in the distance. It is peaceful, romantic, and truly feels like you are floating above the domes.

Do I need to rent a car?+

We strongly recommend against renting a car. The roads on the Amalfi Coast are extremely narrow, winding, and crowded. Parking is scarce and expensive. We have arranged transportation for the wedding events, and for your free time, we recommend hiring private drivers or using ferries.
